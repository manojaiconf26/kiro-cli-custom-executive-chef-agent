# Prep List: Idli

## Ingredients Prep
| Ingredient | Measurement | Preparation Action |
|---|---|---|
| Idli rice (parboiled rice) | 2 cups | Wash thoroughly in 2–3 changes of water, soak with fenugreek seeds in water for 4–6 hours |
| Whole urad dal (black gram, skinned) | 1 cup | Wash thoroughly, soak separately in water for 4–6 hours |
| Fenugreek seeds | 1/2 teaspoon | Soak along with rice |
| Salt | 1/2 teaspoon | Measure and set aside |
| Water | As needed | Keep 2 cups ready for grinding, plus extra for batter consistency |

## Steps
1. Wash 2 cups idli rice and 1 cup urad dal separately under running water until water runs clear.
2. Soak rice with 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds in 4 cups water for 4–6 hours. Soak urad dal separately in 2 cups water for 4–6 hours.
3. Drain urad dal. Grind in a wet grinder or mixer with 1/4 cup water at a time (total ~1/2 cup) to a smooth, fluffy batter — approximately 15–20 minutes in a wet grinder. Transfer to a large vessel.
4. Drain rice. Grind to a slightly coarse batter with ~3/4 cup water — approximately 10–15 minutes. Combine with urad dal batter.
5. Add 1/2 teaspoon salt. Mix well by hand using circular motion for 2–3 minutes to incorporate air.
6. Cover vessel with a lid and ferment in a warm place (28–30°C) for 8–12 hours until batter doubles in volume.
7. Gently stir fermented batter 3–4 times without deflating. Add 1–2 tablespoons water if too thick.
8. Grease idli moulds with a few drops of sesame oil.
9. Pour batter into each mould, filling three-quarters full.
10. Steam in idli steamer for 10–12 minutes. Test with toothpick — should come out clean.
11. Rest 1 minute, scoop out with wet spoon. Serve immediately.

# Prep List: Tiffin Sambar

## Ingredients Prep
| Ingredient | Measurement | Preparation Action |
|---|---|---|
| Toor dal | 1/2 cup | Wash in 2–3 changes of water, drain |
| Shallot (sambar onion) | 1 small (about 20g) | Peel and quarter |
| Tomato | 1 small (about 50g) | Wash and chop into small pieces |
| Drumstick | 1/2 cup (about 2 pieces) | Wash, trim ends, cut into 2-inch pieces |
| Amchur (dry mango powder) | 1 teaspoon | Measure and set aside (dry powder, no soaking needed) |
| Turmeric powder | 1/4 teaspoon | Measure and set aside |
| Coconut oil | 1 tablespoon | Measure and set aside |
| Coriander seeds | 1 tablespoon | Measure for dry roasting |
| Cumin seeds | 1 teaspoon | Measure for dry roasting |
| Black peppercorns | 1/2 teaspoon | Measure for dry roasting |
| Fenugreek seeds | 1/2 teaspoon | Measure for dry roasting |
| Dried red chilies (for spice powder) | 4 pieces | Set aside for dry roasting |
| Chana dal | 1 teaspoon | Measure for dry roasting |
| Urad dal (for spice powder) | 1 teaspoon | Measure for dry roasting |
| Asafoetida (hing) | 1 small marble-sized ball | Set aside for dry roasting |
| Mustard seeds | 1 teaspoon | Measure for tempering |
| Urad dal (for tempering) | 1/2 teaspoon | Measure for tempering |
| Curry leaves | 10 leaves | Wash and pat dry |
| Dried red chilies (for tempering) | 2 pieces | Set aside for tempering |
| Salt | 1/2 teaspoon | Measure and set aside |

## Steps
1. Wash 1/2 cup toor dal in 2–3 changes of water. Add to pressure cooker with 1.5 cups water and a pinch of turmeric. Cook for 3 whistles (about 15 minutes). Mash smooth with a ladle.
2. Dry roast coriander seeds, cumin seeds, black peppercorns, fenugreek seeds, 4 dried red chilies, chana dal, urad dal, and asafoetida in a dry pan on low heat for 3–4 minutes, stirring continuously until fragrant and lightly browned. Cool for 5 minutes. Grind to a fine powder in a spice grinder.
3. No tamarind soaking needed — amchur is added dry during cooking.
4. Heat 1 tablespoon coconut oil in a heavy-bottomed pan over medium heat. Add 1 teaspoon mustard seeds, wait for spluttering (about 30 seconds). Add 1/2 teaspoon urad dal, fry until golden (about 1 minute). Add 10 curry leaves and 2 dried red chilies, sauté 30 seconds.
5. Add quartered shallots, sauté for 2 minutes until translucent. Add chopped tomato, cook for 3 minutes until soft and mushy.
6. Add drumstick pieces with 1/2 cup water. Cover and cook for 5–7 minutes until drumstick is tender when pierced with a fork.
7. Add 1 teaspoon amchur, remaining turmeric (about 1/4 teaspoon), and 1/2 teaspoon salt. Stir well, bring to a boil, then simmer for 5 minutes.
8. Add 2 tablespoons of the freshly ground spice powder. Stir well and simmer for 3 minutes.
9. Add mashed toor dal. Add 1/2–1 cup water to thin the sambar — it should be noticeably thinner than regular sambar, flowing easily off a spoon.
10. Simmer on low heat for 5 minutes. Taste and adjust salt. Serve hot alongside idli.
