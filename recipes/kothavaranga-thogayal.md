# Kothavaranga Thogayal

## Regional Classification
Tamil Nadu

## Dietary Tags
Vegetarian, Vegan, Gluten-Free

## Ingredients
- 1 cup kothavaranga (cluster beans), chopped into small pieces
- 1 small onion, roughly chopped
- 1 tablespoon chana dal
- 2 dried red chilies
- 1 small marble-sized tamarind
- 2 tablespoons desiccated coconut, rehydrated in warm water
- 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
- 5 curry leaves
- 2 teaspoons sesame oil (gingelly oil)
- Salt to taste

## Substitutions Applied
- Urad dal → Chana dal (1:1 ratio; slightly different texture in tempering)
- Fresh grated coconut (1/4 cup) → Desiccated coconut (2 tablespoons, rehydrated; 0.5x conversion ratio)

## Steps
1. Soak 2 tablespoons desiccated coconut in warm water for 10 minutes, drain
2. Heat 1 teaspoon sesame oil in a pan, roast chana dal until golden brown, set aside
3. In the same pan, roast dried red chilies for 30 seconds, set aside
4. Add the chopped kothavaranga and sauté on medium heat for 5–6 minutes until cooked through and slightly browned
5. Add the chopped onion and sauté for 2 minutes until translucent
6. Let everything cool to room temperature
7. In a mixie (blender), combine roasted chana dal, red chilies, tamarind, rehydrated desiccated coconut, cooked kothavaranga, onion, and salt
8. Grind to a coarse, thick paste — add 1–2 tablespoons of water only if needed
9. Heat remaining 1 teaspoon sesame oil, splutter mustard seeds, add curry leaves, pour over the thogayal
10. Mix well and serve with hot steamed rice and a drizzle of sesame oil
