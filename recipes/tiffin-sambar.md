# Tiffin Sambar

## Regional Classification
Tamil Nadu

## Dietary Tags
Vegetarian, Vegan, Gluten-Free

## Ingredients
- 1/2 cup toor dal
- 1 small shallot (sambar onion), quartered
- 1 small tomato, chopped
- 1/2 cup drumstick, cut into 2-inch pieces
- 1 teaspoon amchur (dry mango powder)
- 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
- 1 tablespoon coconut oil
- Salt to taste

### Whole Spice Roast (dry roast and grind to powder)
- 1 tablespoon coriander seeds
- 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
- 1/2 teaspoon black peppercorns
- 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
- 4 dried red chilies
- 1 teaspoon chana dal
- 1 teaspoon urad dal
- 1 small marble-sized ball of asafoetida (hing)

### Tempering
- 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
- 1/2 teaspoon urad dal
- 10 curry leaves
- 2 dried red chilies

## Substitutions Applied
Tamarind paste → Amchur / dry mango powder (ratio: 1:0.3) — 1 tablespoon tamarind paste replaced with 1 teaspoon amchur

## Steps
1. Wash toor dal and pressure cook with 1.5 cups water and a pinch of turmeric for 3 whistles. Mash smooth and set aside.
2. Dry roast coriander seeds, cumin seeds, black peppercorns, fenugreek seeds, dried red chilies, chana dal, urad dal, and asafoetida in a pan on low heat until fragrant and lightly browned (3–4 minutes). Cool and grind to a fine powder.
3. No tamarind soaking needed — amchur is a dry powder added directly during cooking.
4. In a heavy-bottomed pan, heat coconut oil. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add urad dal, fry until golden. Add curry leaves and dried red chilies, sauté 30 seconds.
5. Add shallots and sauté until translucent. Add tomato and cook until soft.
6. Add drumstick pieces with 1/2 cup water. Cook covered for 5–7 minutes until drumstick is tender.
7. Add 1 teaspoon amchur, remaining turmeric, and salt. Stir well, bring to a boil and simmer 5 minutes.
8. Add 2 tablespoons of the freshly ground spice powder. Stir well and simmer 3 minutes.
9. Add mashed toor dal. Adjust consistency with water — tiffin sambar should be thinner than regular sambar, flowing easily for dipping.
10. Simmer on low heat for 5 minutes. The sambar should be aromatic, tangy, and light-bodied. Serve hot alongside idli.
