# Idli

## Regional Classification
Tamil Nadu

## Dietary Tags
Vegetarian, Vegan, Gluten-Free

## Ingredients
- 2 cups idli rice (parboiled rice)
- 1 cup whole urad dal (black gram, skinned)
- 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
- Salt to taste
- Water as needed for grinding and batter consistency

## Substitutions Applied
None

## Steps
1. Wash idli rice and urad dal separately. Soak rice with fenugreek seeds in water for 4–6 hours. Soak urad dal separately for 4–6 hours.
2. Grind urad dal first with minimal water to a smooth, fluffy batter. Transfer to a large vessel.
3. Grind soaked rice to a slightly coarse batter. Combine with the urad dal batter.
4. Add salt and mix well by hand using a circular motion. The batter should be thick and pourable.
5. Cover and ferment in a warm place for 8–12 hours or overnight until the batter doubles in volume and turns slightly sour.
6. Gently stir the fermented batter without deflating it too much. Adjust consistency with a splash of water if needed — it should flow off a ladle slowly.
7. Grease idli moulds with a few drops of sesame oil or water.
8. Pour batter into each mould, filling about three-quarters full.
9. Steam in an idli steamer or pressure cooker (without the weight) for 10–12 minutes until a toothpick inserted comes out clean.
10. Let rest for 1 minute, then scoop out idlis with a wet spoon. Serve hot with tiffin sambar and coconut chutney.
